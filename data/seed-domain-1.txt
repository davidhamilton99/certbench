Sub-objective: 1.1 — Compare and contrast various types of security controls
Question: Which category of security control is best described as a policy, procedure, or guideline that is implemented by people rather than technology or physical means?
A) Technical control
B) Physical control
C) Managerial control
D) Operational control
Correct: D
Explanation: Operational controls are implemented and executed by people through procedures, training, and day-to-day security practices rather than through technology or physical barriers. Managerial controls are often confused here, but managerial controls focus on oversight, risk management, and governance — the administrative framework that guides security decisions. Operational controls are the human-executed activities that carry out the intent of managerial controls.

A) Preventive
B) Corrective
C) Detective
D) Deterrent
Correct: D
Explanation: Deterrent controls are designed to discourage potential attackers from attempting to breach security by making the environment appear risky or well-monitored, without necessarily stopping an attack if one is attempted. Preventive controls would actually block unauthorised access, whereas deterrent controls rely on psychological discouragement. Lighting and warning signs are classic deterrent examples used in physical security.

Question: A user sends a digitally signed email to a colleague. Several months later, a dispute arises about whether the user actually sent the message. The digital signature on the email is used as proof that the user sent it. Which security principle does this scenario demonstrate?
A) Integrity
B) Confidentiality
C) Non-repudiation
D) Availability
Correct: C
Explanation: Non-repudiation ensures that a party cannot deny having performed an action, such as sending a message, because cryptographic evidence (a digital signature) ties the action to their identity in a way they cannot plausibly dispute. Integrity ensures data has not been altered in transit, which is different from proving origin. Digital signatures provide both integrity and non-repudiation simultaneously, but the key principle illustrated by inability to deny authorship is non-repudiation.
