Question: A technician needs to upgrade the RAM in a laptop. After opening the bottom panel, they find the RAM modules are installed at an angle and held in place by metal clips. What is the correct procedure to remove the existing RAM?
A) Pull the module straight out along the slot's axis
B) Press the retaining clips outward until the module pops up, then slide it out at an angle
C) Unscrew the module from the motherboard before pulling it out
D) Twist the module 90 degrees and then pull it straight up
Correct: B
Explanation: Laptop SO-DIMM RAM is installed at roughly a 45-degree angle and secured by spring-loaded retaining clips on each side; pressing those clips outward releases the module so it pops up to the insertion angle and can be slid out. Pulling the module straight out along the slot axis would damage the contacts or break the slot. This lever-and-angle mechanism is the same for both DDR4 and DDR5 SO-DIMMs found in modern laptops.
