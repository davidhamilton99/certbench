Explanation: Telnet (e.g., `telnet servername 443`) or PowerShell's Test-NetConnection (e.g., `Test-NetConnection -ComputerName server -Port 443`) can test TCP port connectivity to a remote host. When ping succeeds but a service is unreachable, port testing with telnet or Test-NetConnection confirms whether the specific service port is accessible through the firewall.

Question: A user reports their SSD-equipped laptop boots significantly slower than it used to. The SSD health tools show the drive is 98% full. What is the MOST likely cause of the slowdown?
A) The SSD's NAND flash cells are worn out
B) The SSD has insufficient free space for wear leveling and garbage collection operations
C) The SATA interface is operating in IDE mode instead of AHCI
D) The SSD's firmware is outdated
Correct: B
Explanation: SSDs require free space to perform wear leveling, garbage collection, and TRIM operations; when a drive is near capacity, these background processes are impaired, causing significant performance degradation. Worn NAND cells typically show up in S.M.A.R.T. data as increased error counts, not simply slow boot times. Keep SSDs below 80-85% capacity to maintain optimal performance — the last 15-20% of SSD capacity is critical for background maintenance operations.

B) A second drive failure would result in complete data loss
C) The RAID controller will corrupt the rebuild
